About Me

Hi, I'm Kevin Mason and I'm a fourth year co-op student at the University of British Columbia majoring in both Business and Computer Science (BUCS). The BUCS program has been the perfect blend of my interests in both commerce and computer science. After four years in the program, my dream is to be one of the people that bridge the gap between the technical aspects of technology, and the strategic side of business.

Aside from academia and coding, I define myself as an athlete. For the past 13 years, I've been a competitive swimmer at WRASA, holding 3 club records and earning top ten placings of BCSSA's Top 60 Swims of All Time. During the past 5 years, I dedicated my time to giving back to the club through coaching.

During my final year, at the end of the 2014 season, I was inducted into the WRASA Hall of Fame. The award recognizes individuals who have made outstanding contributions to WRASA as both a swimmer and as a coach, resulting in the strengthening and advancement of WRASA. You can watch my induction video here.

  • Yelp.ca Redesign

    During my Human Computer Interaction course at UBC (CPSC 344), I worked in a team of four to propose a new interface for Yelp.ca. We conducted studies to analyze consumer behaviour through co-discovery observational studies and determine specific issues and frustrations. We then developed conceptual models that matched the user's mental model before creating a medium-fidelity HTML prototype using web front-end design where the widgets and filters were redesigned to match the user's original critique of Yelp.ca.

    I was primarily responsible for designing the co-discovery study in the first iteration, the wizard-based paper prototype in the second iteration, and developing the HTML medium-fidelity prototype in the third iteration (seen here).

    Here is a link to our Map and Wizard-based paper prototypes. You can also check out our Git repo here.

  • Cal's Music Store

    During my database course at UBC (CPSC 304), I worked in a team of four to implement an online music store. We analyzed user requirements, developed ER diagrams to determine how to best represent the user's requirements, and used MySQL to implement our tables and initialize the store's data. I was in charge of implementing the UI and the search functionality, using HTML and PHP to interact with and display the database. The project was a great learning experience, working with the UI, logic, and database layers of software architecture.

    You can check out our Git repo here.

  • Restaurant Randomizer

    Currently in CPSC 310 (Software Engineering), I'm working in a team of four to implement a web application using GWT and Google Places API. Our application is geared to provide a tinder-like suggestion interface for deciding which restaurants to eat at, and providing information about that restaurant to the user.

  • Volunteer Leadership Program

    As the Head Coach of WRASA, one of the projects I am most proud of is founding the Volunteer Leadership Program in 2013. The Volunteer Leadership Program aims to provide leadership opportunities for senior swimmers (ages 14-18) interested in giving back to the club, and potentially become coaches themselves. After two years, it has recruited more swimmers into coaches than any previous program to date. Planning, initiating, and implementing the program was extremely rewarding as I saw an active change in the leadership of our older swimmers, and a future team of coaches that would succeed my own position.
